# Commercial Use & Enterprise Licensing

This project is released under the [Intelligaia Source Available License (ISAL) v1.0](./LICENSE). It is **source-available**, not OSI-approved open source — some commercial uses require a separate license.

This document explains, in plain language, what is and isn't allowed without contacting us, and how to get an enterprise license when you need one.

## What you can do without contacting us

- **Evaluate** the project — clone it, run it, read the code.
- **Learn from it** — use it in coursework, blog posts, conference talks, research.
- **Self-host internally** — run it inside your own organization for your own employees (e.g., as your internal employee directory).
- **Fork and modify** it for any of the above purposes.
- **Contribute back** — open issues, send PRs, share patches.
- **Build prototypes and demos** that aren't sold commercially.

Internal use means: your organization is the user, your employees are the audience, and you aren't billing customers for the project's functionality.

## What requires a commercial license

You need a written agreement with Intelligaia before you do any of the following:

- **Offer the project as a hosted / managed service** to customers (SaaS, PaaS, or otherwise).
- **Bundle it into a paid product** where the project provides a substantial portion of the product's value.
- **White-label or rebrand** the project for commercial customers.
- **Sell, sublicense, or redistribute** the project or derivatives commercially.
- **Use the project to build a competing platform** in the employee-profile, digital-business-card, or related categories.
- **Use the project's premium assets** (illustrations, design templates, brand visual systems) outside the limits in the LICENSE.
- **Train ML / AI models** on the project's source code, prompts, workflows, documentation, or design assets (see LICENSE Section 3).

If you aren't sure which side of the line your use case falls on, ask. We'd rather have the conversation early than untangle it later.

## How enterprise licensing works

A commercial license typically covers:

- The specific scope of your commercial use (SaaS, white-label, embedded, training, etc.).
- A grant of rights beyond what ISAL provides.
- Optional add-ons: priority support, security SLAs, contractual indemnity, feature roadmap input, private patches.
- Term, pricing, and renewal terms.

We try to keep licensing simple and proportional to your scale.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I use this for my own company's internal employee directory?**
Yes. Internal, non-resale use is permitted under ISAL. You still need to keep the "Powered by Intelligaia" attribution on public-facing pages.

**Q: Can I remove the attribution?**
Not without a commercial license. The attribution requirement is Section 4 of the LICENSE.

**Q: Can I sell consulting services around this project?**
Selling your *services* (setup, hosting in the customer's infra, customization) is fine. Selling *the project itself* — or a hosted service whose core value is the project — is not, without a commercial license.

**Q: Can I publish a fork on GitHub?**
Yes, provided you keep the LICENSE file intact, don't use the Intelligaia name in your fork's branding (see [TRADEMARK.md](./TRADEMARK.md)), and your fork itself is also under ISAL or a compatible license.

**Q: What happens after a commercial agreement is signed?**
You get the rights described in the agreement, typically a separate license document or a rider to ISAL. The public repo stays under ISAL — the agreement governs *your* use, not the project's overall license.
