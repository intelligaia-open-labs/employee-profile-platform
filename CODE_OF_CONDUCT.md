# Intelligaia Community Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

At Intelligaia, we are building an open and collaborative ecosystem around AI-native experiences, agentic systems, design systems, developer tooling, research, and emerging interaction paradigms.

We are committed to creating a community that is:

- respectful,
- inclusive,
- constructive,
- safe for learning,
- professionally collaborative,
- and welcoming to contributors of all backgrounds and experience levels.

This Code of Conduct applies across:

- GitHub repositories,
- discussions,
- pull requests,
- issues,
- documentation,
- design reviews,
- community forums,
- events,
- presentations,
- and all public collaboration spaces operated by Intelligaia.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contribute to a positive environment include:

- Being respectful in communication and feedback.
- Welcoming new contributors and different perspectives.
- Giving constructive criticism professionally.
- Focusing on ideas, not individuals.
- Sharing knowledge openly and responsibly.
- Supporting accessibility and inclusive design practices.
- Respecting community guidelines and project boundaries.
- Helping maintain a collaborative and research-driven culture.

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are not tolerated:

- Harassment, discrimination, or hate speech.
- Personal attacks, insults, or intimidation.
- Trolling, inflammatory comments, or deliberate disruption.
- Publishing private or confidential information without permission.
- Spam, malicious links, or deceptive behavior.
- Plagiarism or intentional misrepresentation of work.
- Unauthorized commercial promotion or solicitation.
- Uploading malicious code, harmful scripts, or security exploits.
- Attempting to bypass licensing, attribution, or repository protections.
- Using community spaces to attack individuals, organizations, or contributors.

## AI and Responsible Usage

Because Intelligaia works extensively in AI-native systems and agentic experiences, contributors are expected to use AI responsibly.

Contributors should:

- clearly disclose significant AI-generated contributions when appropriate,
- avoid submitting misleading synthetic content,
- avoid generating harmful, deceptive, or malicious outputs,
- respect intellectual property and attribution requirements,
- avoid training or extracting datasets from protected community assets without permission.

## Design and Research Respect

We value:

- original thinking,
- experimentation,
- collaborative critique,
- and responsible adaptation.

Contributors should not:

- directly copy proprietary work from other organizations,
- submit stolen design assets,
- impersonate brands or creators,
- or misuse shared research materials.

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for:

- clarifying and enforcing community standards,
- reviewing reports fairly,
- taking appropriate corrective action,
- and maintaining a respectful collaboration environment.

Maintainers may remove, edit, or reject:

- comments,
- commits,
- issues,
- pull requests,
- assets,
- or contributions

that violate this Code of Conduct.

## Enforcement Actions

Depending on severity, violations may result in:

1. Warning
2. Temporary restriction from discussions or repositories
3. Removal of contributed content
4. Suspension from contribution access
5. Permanent ban from community participation
6. Legal escalation in severe abuse, fraud, or IP misuse cases

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it to:

- **opensource@intelligaia.com**

or

- **legal@intelligaia.com**

Please include:

- repository / project name,
- relevant links or screenshots,
- and a brief description of the issue.

All reports will be reviewed confidentially and professionally.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within:

- project repositories,
- official communication channels,
- issue discussions,
- pull requests,
- design collaboration spaces,
- community events,
- and any space where an individual represents Intelligaia or its projects.

## Attribution

This Code of Conduct is inspired by:

- the [Contributor Covenant](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/),
- Open Source community standards,
- and modern AI collaboration governance practices,

and adapted for Intelligaia's AI-native ecosystem.

## Final Principle

We believe the future of AI-native systems should be:

- human-centered,
- transparent,
- collaborative,
- and responsibly designed.

Every contributor helps shape that future.
