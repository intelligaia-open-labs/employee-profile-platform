## Design Context

### Users
Corporate and enterprise employees who share digital business profiles at conferences, client meetings, and professional events. Admins (HR or ops managers) create and manage employee profiles through a dashboard. The public-facing profile is viewed by clients, partners, and contacts — typically on a phone after scanning a QR code, often in bright environments (event halls, offices, lobbies).

### Brand Personality
**Bold, Confident, Premium.** This is a tool that represents a company's people — it needs to feel as polished as the organization behind it. Every interaction should communicate competence and authority. Think: the digital equivalent of a heavy-stock embossed business card.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: High-end minimal with deliberate boldness. Inspired by Apple and Linear — generous whitespace, every element considered, nothing decorative without purpose. Strong typographic hierarchy does the heavy lifting.
- **Theme**: Light mode. Profiles are scanned on phones in daylight — bright, open, confident.
- **Anti-references**: Avoid generic SaaS dashboards, cookie-cutter card layouts, or anything that feels like a template. No playful illustrations or startup-y gradients.
- **Differentiator**: The public profile should feel like a personal brand statement, not a database record. It should make the person behind it look important.

### Design Principles
1. **Authority through restraint** — Fewer elements, each one commanding attention. Whitespace is a power move, not wasted space.
2. **Typography-first hierarchy** — Type size, weight, and spacing do the organizational work. Minimize reliance on borders, boxes, and color to create structure.
3. **Purposeful polish** — Every detail (spacing, alignment, transitions) should feel considered. The difference between "fine" and "impressive" is in the micro-details.
4. **Phone-first public experience** — The profile page is primarily viewed on mobile after a QR scan. Design for that moment: fast load, immediate comprehension, easy action.
5. **Professional gravitas** — The interface should make users feel like they're using something built for serious organizations, not a side project.
